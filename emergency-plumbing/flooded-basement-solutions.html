First, ensure safety by turning off electricity to the basement. Next, wear protective gear, such as gloves and boots, and assess the extent of flooding. Remove valuable items that haven't been affected by the water and then contact a professional for help if needed.

Use a wet/dry vacuum for small amounts of water or, for larger volumes, a sump pump or a utility pump can be effective. Make sure to direct the water far away from your home's foundation.

Common causes include heavy rainfall, melting snow, burst pipes, poor drainage, and foundation cracks. Identifying the cause is crucial to preventing future flooding.

Remove all wet items and thoroughly dry the area using fans and dehumidifiers. Disinfect surfaces with a bleach solution and consider using anti-mold products. It's important to act quickly, as mold can begin growing within 24-48 hours.

Ensure your gutters and downspouts are clean and direct water away from your home. Consider installing a sump pump with a battery backup, sealing basement walls, and improving the grading around your home to direct water away from the foundation.

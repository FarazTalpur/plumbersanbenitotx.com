Frequently Asked Questions

The basic steps include turning off the water supply, connecting the tubing to the water supply, routing the tubing to the refrigerator, connecting the tubing to the ice maker, and ensuring all connections are secure without leaks.

You will need a water line installation kit, a drill, a wrench, a screwdriver, a shutoff valve, tubing (copper or PEX), and Teflon tape. Always check the specific requirements for your ice maker model.

If you have basic plumbing skills, you can install an ice maker line yourself. However, if you're unsure, hiring a professional can ensure the installation is done correctly without potential water damage.

Most ice makers require water pressure between 40 and 120 PSI. You can measure your homeâ€™s water pressure using a pressure gauge attached to an outdoor hose bib or consult with a plumber.

If you notice leaks, check all connections to ensure they are tight and properly sealed. Use a wrench to tighten any loose fittings and apply Teflon tape if necessary. If leaks persist, consider consulting a professional.
